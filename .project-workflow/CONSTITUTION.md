# Constitution

## Mission

- Create an 8-bit Pac-Man-inspired arcade game where players can enjoy the familiar maze-chase loop with custom characters that make the experience feel personal, replayable, and easy to share.

## Target Users

- Players who want a fast, approachable browser arcade game with recognizable Pac-Man-style rules and playful character customization.
- Creators, friends, or small groups who want to swap in custom player and enemy characters without changing the core game design.
- The project owner, who needs a clear workflow for shaping the game from concept through playable milestones.

## Core Outcomes

- Players can start a game quickly, understand the objective immediately, and complete a satisfying round without needing instructions.
- The maze, movement, dots, power-ups, enemies, score, lives, win state, and loss state work together as a coherent arcade loop.
- Custom characters are a first-class part of the product, not a late cosmetic layer.
- Character customization supports distinct player and enemy identities while preserving readable gameplay and fair challenge.
- The visual style feels intentionally 8-bit: simple, bold, low-detail, and readable rather than modern or graphically elaborate.
- The game remains responsive and legible across common desktop and mobile browser sizes.
- Each development milestone produces a playable, testable improvement rather than only internal scaffolding.

## Product Principles

- Familiar first: preserve the clarity and immediacy of classic maze-chase gameplay.
- Customization with purpose: character changes should add personality without making movement, collision, or enemy states harder to read.
- 8-bit restraint: favor pixel-art shapes, limited detail, crisp edges, and strong silhouettes over fancy graphics or visual effects.
- Feel matters: input response, animation timing, collision behavior, and audio feedback are core product quality, not polish-only extras.
- Small playable increments: prefer narrow slices that can be played, validated, and improved.
- Accessible fun: prioritize clear contrast, readable states, forgiving onboarding, and controls that do not require expert timing to enjoy.
- Shareable identity: make it easy for the game to feel like "our version" through characters, names, and visual themes.

## Non-Goals

- Recreating the original Pac-Man exactly, including every historical timing rule, maze quirk, or ghost algorithm.
- Building a general-purpose game engine before proving the playable game loop.
- Optimizing for complex multiplayer, accounts, online leaderboards, or monetization in the first product phase.
- Pursuing fancy, high-fidelity, 3D, realistic, or heavily animated graphics.
- Letting visual customization compromise fair collision, readable enemy behavior, or level clarity.
- Treating the project constitution as a place for framework choices, file layouts, lint rules, or other implementation directives.
